Topos of Trees

The topos of trees, Psh(ω), classifies the theory of eventually-true monotonic sequences of propositions. n:Nn:N,Pn​​⊢Pn​:Prop⊢Pn+1​⊢∃n:N,Pn​​

Properties

  • Localic: The theory is propositional.

Morphisms

Global Sections

Endomorphisms

There is an adjoint triple of endofunctors given by the following model morphisms: (P0​Pn+1​​:=⊥:=Pn​​)⊣(Pn​​:=Pn+1​​)⊣(P0​Pn+1​​:=P0​:=Pn​​) This exhibits the topos of trees as a cohesive topos over itself.

The middle morphism has a natural transformation from the identity. The outer two have natural transformations to the identity.

The direct image of the leftmost morphism is the later modality, written ▹. The natural transformations mentioned above yield a function next:A→▹A.

Theorem: Internally to the topos of trees, there is a function fix:(▹A→A)→A, satisfying fix(f)=f(next(fix(f))).
